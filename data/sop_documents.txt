WAREHOUSE STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (SOPs)
================================================

SOP-001: INBOUND RECEIVING PROCEDURE
--------------------------------------
Document ID: WH-SOP-001
Version: 2.1
Department: Inbound / Receiving
Applies to: Receiving Clerks, Dock Supervisors, Warehouse Associates

OBJECTIVE:
To ensure all incoming shipments are received, inspected, and recorded accurately into the Warehouse Management System (WMS) without delay.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1 - Pre-Arrival Preparation
1.1. Log into WMS and review the Expected Receipts list for the day.
1.2. Confirm Purchase Orders (POs) and Advance Shipping Notices (ASNs) are available.
1.3. Assign available dock doors to incoming carriers based on appointment schedule.
1.4. Ensure receiving equipment is ready: forklifts, pallet jacks, scanners, label printers.
1.5. Allocate receiving staff based on expected volume.

Step 2 - Carrier Arrival and Dock Assignment
2.1. Carrier checks in at the security gate with delivery documentation.
2.2. Security verifies the carrier identity and records details (truck number, driver ID, carrier name).
2.3. Dock coordinator assigns an available dock door and directs the carrier.
2.4. Carrier backs truck into dock. Dock leveler and wheel chocks are applied.

Step 3 - Documentation Verification
3.1. Receiving clerk collects the Bill of Lading (BOL) and Packing List from the driver.
3.2. Verify supplier name, PO number, and expected SKU list against WMS.
3.3. Note any seal number on the trailer. Verify seal is intact before breaking.
3.4. Record any discrepancy in delivery documentation before unloading begins.

Step 4 - Unloading
4.1. Unload goods using appropriate equipment (forklift for pallets, hand trucks for cartons).
4.2. Count total pallets/cartons as they are unloaded.
4.3. Place goods in the designated staging area in the receiving dock.
4.4. Do not place goods in storage aisles until inspection is complete.

Step 5 - Inspection
5.1. Verify outer carton/pallet count against BOL.
5.2. Randomly open cartons (10% sample minimum) to verify:
     - Correct SKU / product code
     - Quantity per carton
     - Product condition (no damage, no leakage)
     - Expiry dates (if applicable)
     - Correct labeling and barcodes
5.3. For full inspection orders (as flagged in WMS), open 100% of cartons.
5.4. Record all findings (quantities confirmed, shortages, overages, damages).

Step 6 - GRN Creation
6.1. Enter received quantities into WMS for each SKU.
6.2. Generate the Goods Receipt Note (GRN) in WMS.
6.3. Print and attach GRN confirmation labels to received pallets.
6.4. For discrepancies: Create Discrepancy Report in WMS and email procurement and supplier.

Step 7 - Put-Away
7.1. WMS generates put-away task with assigned bin location for each SKU.
7.2. Forklift operator scans GRN label and follows WMS put-away instructions.
7.3. Scan shelf barcode at destination location to confirm put-away.
7.4. WMS updates inventory balance in real time.

Step 8 - Completion
8.1. Carrier driver signs GRN acknowledgement (or electronic confirmation via carrier app).
8.2. Driver is released. Dock doors are secured.
8.3. Receiving supervisor reviews GRN report for accuracy.
8.4. GRN documents archived in WMS and sent to accounts payable for invoice matching.

EXCEPTION HANDLING:
- Damaged goods: Move to quarantine zone, photograph, create damage report, contact supplier
- Short shipment: Record actual qty, contact supplier for credit or re-delivery
- Over-shipment: Record overage, hold excess in staging, contact procurement for authorization to accept
- Wrong goods: Do not put-away, create RTV (Return to Vendor) order in WMS

KPIs TRACKED BY THIS SOP:
- Dock-to-Stock Cycle Time (target: <4 hours)
- Receiving Accuracy Rate (target: >99%)
- GRN Processing Time


SOP-002: STORAGE AND PUT-AWAY PROCEDURE
-----------------------------------------
Document ID: WH-SOP-002
Version: 1.8
Department: Storage / Inventory Management

OBJECTIVE:
To ensure all received goods are stored in the correct, designated locations as directed by the WMS, maintaining inventory accuracy and optimizing warehouse space.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1 - Receive Put-Away Task
1.1. Upon GRN completion, WMS automatically assigns put-away tasks to available associates.
1.2. Picker receives task on RF scanner or voice system: SKU, quantity, destination bin.
1.3. Associate confirms task acceptance in WMS.

Step 2 - Navigate to Source Location
2.1. Associate collects goods from the receiving staging area.
2.2. Verify goods match task: scan pallet label or product barcode.
2.3. WMS confirms correct item and quantity.

Step 3 - Navigate to Destination
3.1. Follow WMS-directed path to destination bin location.
3.2. Confirm aisle, bay, level, and position match WMS instruction.

Step 4 - Confirm Put-Away
4.1. Scan destination shelf barcode.
4.2. WMS verifies location is correct and available.
4.3. Place goods in location carefully; do not overfill or overhang shelf.
4.4. Confirm put-away quantity in WMS.
4.5. WMS updates live inventory balance.

Step 5 - Exception Handling
5.1. If bin is full: alert supervisor; WMS provides alternative location.
5.2. If goods do not fit: report to supervisor for slotting review.
5.3. If wrong goods scanned: abort task, report discrepancy to supervisor.

FIFO COMPLIANCE:
- All perishable, pharmaceutical, and dated goods must follow FIFO rotation.
- When system directs put-away to a mixed-date bin, ensure older stock is front-facing and accessible.
- Verify lot numbers / expiry dates are scanned and recorded in WMS.


SOP-003: ORDER PICKING PROCEDURE
----------------------------------
Document ID: WH-SOP-003
Version: 3.0
Department: Picking / Fulfillment

OBJECTIVE:
To accurately and efficiently pick customer orders from storage locations, minimizing errors and maximizing throughput.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1 - Receive Pick Task
1.1. Picker logs into WMS via RF scanner, voice terminal, or PTL system.
1.2. WMS assigns pick tasks based on wave or batch release.
1.3. Picker collects appropriate picking equipment: cart, tote, pallet, labels.

Step 2 - Navigate to Pick Location
2.1. Follow WMS-directed path optimized for travel efficiency.
2.2. Confirm aisle, bay, level, and position match WMS instruction.

Step 3 - Pick Execution
3.1. Scan shelf barcode to confirm correct location.
3.2. Scan product barcode to confirm correct SKU.
3.3. Pick the exact quantity instructed by WMS.
3.4. Confirm pick in WMS (button press on PTL, voice confirmation, or scanner input).
3.5. Place picked items in correct order tote (for batch/cluster picking, tote is labeled with order number).

Step 4 - Short Pick Handling
4.1. If insufficient stock at pick face: record short pick in WMS.
4.2. WMS directs to alternate location or reserve stock.
4.3. If still unavailable: picker alerts supervisor; supervisor reviews stock availability.

Step 5 - Pick Confirmation
5.1. After completing all pick tasks for the wave: picker delivers goods to packing station.
5.2. Scan tote barcode at packing station handoff.
5.3. WMS transfers custody of order from picking to packing.

Step 6 - Replenishment Trigger
6.1. When pick face stock falls below minimum level, WMS generates a replenishment task.
6.2. Replenishment associate refills pick face from reserve storage.
6.3. FIFO must be maintained during replenishment (older stock at front).

PICK QUALITY RULES:
- Never pick damaged goods; mark as damaged and alert supervisor
- Never skip a location without recording in WMS
- Do not leave totes unattended in aisles


SOP-004: PACKING AND LABELING PROCEDURE
-----------------------------------------
Document ID: WH-SOP-004
Version: 2.5
Department: Packing / Dispatch Preparation

OBJECTIVE:
To securely pack customer orders and apply accurate shipping labels in preparation for dispatch.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1 - Order Verification
1.1. Packing associate receives order tote at packing station.
1.2. Scan tote barcode; WMS displays order contents.
1.3. Verify all items in tote match order: scan each product barcode.
1.4. Verify quantities match order.
1.5. Visually inspect items for damage. Replace damaged items before packing.

Step 2 - Box Selection
2.1. Select appropriate box size based on product dimensions and fragility.
2.2. WMS cartonization may recommend optimal box size automatically.
2.3. Right-sizing: box should be large enough to fit contents with minimal void, but not excessively large.

Step 3 - Packing
3.1. Line box with suitable cushioning material.
3.2. Place items securely with heaviest items at bottom.
3.3. Fill void space with void fill (air pillows, crumpled paper).
3.4. For fragile items: individually wrap with bubble wrap.
3.5. Ensure no items can shift or move inside the sealed box.
3.6. For multiple-item orders: include packing slip / order confirmation sheet inside box.

Step 4 - Box Sealing
4.1. Fold box flaps correctly.
4.2. Apply H-tape pattern: one strip across center seam, one strip across each end.
4.3. Ensure tape is firmly adhered to all surfaces.

Step 5 - Labeling
5.1. Print shipping label from WMS or carrier system.
5.2. Apply shipping label flat to largest face of box.
5.3. Verify: carrier name, barcode, tracking number, recipient address, sender address.
5.4. Scan tracking barcode to confirm label in WMS.
5.5. Apply special handling labels as required: "Fragile", "This Side Up", etc.
5.6. For international shipments: attach customs invoice and applicable documentation.

Step 6 - Quality Check (QC Gate)
6.1. Final QC associate verifies weight matches expected weight in WMS (±10% tolerance).
6.2. Visually inspect label placement, seal integrity, and box condition.
6.3. Confirm pack in WMS — order status moves to "Dispatched / Ready to Ship".

Step 7 - Outbound Sortation
7.1. Place packed order on outbound conveyor or in designated carrier sortation lane.
7.2. Scanner reads label; WMS sorts to correct carrier lane.


SOP-005: DISPATCH AND SHIPPING PROCEDURE
------------------------------------------
Document ID: WH-SOP-005
Version: 2.0
Department: Dispatch / Shipping

OBJECTIVE:
To ensure all packed orders are loaded into carrier vehicles accurately, documentation is completed, and shipments depart on schedule.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1 - Carrier Arrival
1.1. Carrier trucks arrive as per the dispatching schedule.
1.2. Security logs truck number, carrier name, and arrival time.
1.3. Dock coordinator assigns outbound dock door.

Step 2 - Shipment Manifest Generation
2.1. WMS generates shipping manifest listing all orders allocated to this carrier and truck.
2.2. Manifest includes: order numbers, tracking numbers, weights, dimensions, destinations.
2.3. Manifest is shared electronically with the carrier (EDI or carrier API).
2.4. Printed manifest prepared for physical handoff.

Step 3 - Loading
3.1. Dispatch team organizes packages for loading: last stop loaded first (LIFO loading).
3.2. Scan each package barcode as it is loaded into the truck.
3.3. WMS confirms each scan against manifest; alerts for misloads (wrong truck).
3.4. Heavy packages placed at bottom; fragile packages on top and secured.
3.5. Packages are secured with load bars, straps, or cargo nets.

Step 4 - Documentation Sign-Off
4.1. Carrier driver physically checks loaded packages against manifest.
4.2. Any discrepancy (missing or damaged package) is recorded immediately.
4.3. Driver signs the Bill of Lading (BOL) accepting custody of shipment.
4.4. Warehouse representative retains a signed copy of BOL.

Step 5 - Truck Departure
5.1. Truck is sealed; seal number recorded in WMS.
5.2. Driver's copy of BOL issued.
5.3. Departure time recorded.
5.4. WMS updates all order statuses to "Shipped" and generates tracking numbers.
5.5. Customer shipment confirmation emails triggered by WMS.

Step 6 - Proof of Delivery (POD)
6.1. Once delivered, carrier provides POD (electronic or paper) confirming delivery.
6.2. WMS records POD against order.
6.3. Any failed deliveries or RTS (Return to Sender) shipments are flagged for returns processing.

DISPATCH CUTOFF RULES:
- All packed orders must reach the dispatch staging area 30 minutes before carrier cutoff time
- Late orders are held for next day dispatch unless carrier agrees to wait
- Cutoff times are displayed on dispatch scheduling boards


SOP-006: RETURNS PROCESSING PROCEDURE
---------------------------------------
Document ID: WH-SOP-006
Version: 1.5
Department: Returns / Reverse Logistics

OBJECTIVE:
To process customer returns efficiently and accurately, updating inventory and initiating appropriate disposition actions.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1 - RMA Authorization
1.1. Customer service creates RMA in WMS with: customer order number, SKUs to be returned, reason code, approved quantity.
1.2. Return shipping label with RMA number is provided to customer.

Step 2 - Returns Receipt
2.1. Returns arrive at the Returns Dock (separate from inbound dock).
2.2. Parcel is scanned; WMS retrieves RMA details.
2.3. Associate verifies: correct items, RMA number matches, quantity matches.

Step 3 - Condition Assessment
3.1. Each returned item inspected and graded:
     - Grade A: Sellable — original condition, unopened or like new
     - Grade B: Sellable — opened, tested, functional (refurbished)
     - Grade C: Non-sellable — damaged, incomplete, or expired
3.2. Grade recorded in WMS against each returned SKU.

Step 4 - Disposition
4.1. Grade A → Return to stock: standard put-away process
4.2. Grade B → Refurbishment queue: sent to refurbishment area
4.3. Grade C → Disposition options: donate, destroy, or return to vendor (RTV)
4.4. All disposition actions recorded in WMS.

Step 5 - Customer Refund/Credit
5.1. Returns processing confirmation sent to customer service.
5.2. Customer service initiates refund or store credit per company policy.
5.3. WMS links returns record to customer order for audit trail.
