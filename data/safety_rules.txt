WAREHOUSE SAFETY RULES AND REGULATIONS
=======================================

1. GENERAL WAREHOUSE SAFETY OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------
Warehouse environments are among the most hazard-prone workplaces due to the combination of heavy machinery, moving vehicles, high shelving, manual lifting, and diverse workforce activities occurring simultaneously. Safety in a warehouse is not optional — it is a legal requirement and a moral responsibility.

All warehouse personnel must follow safety rules at all times. Safety rules apply to full-time staff, temporary workers, contractors, and visitors.

Regulatory compliance bodies include:
- OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration) — USA
- HSE (Health and Safety Executive) — UK
- ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems


2. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
----------------------------------------
PPE is the first line of defense for warehouse workers. The correct PPE must be worn at all times in designated areas.

2.1 Mandatory PPE for All Warehouse Areas:
- High-Visibility (Hi-Vis) Vest: Must be worn at all times in active warehouse areas where vehicles operate
- Safety Footwear: Steel-toed boots or shoes are mandatory to protect against dropped goods
- Hard Hat (Helmet): Required in areas with overhead work, crane operations, or racking above 6 meters

2.2 PPE for Specific Tasks:
- Cut-Resistant Gloves: Required when using box cutters, knives, or handling sharp-edged goods
- Safety Glasses / Goggles: Required when operating powered equipment, handling chemicals, cutting
- Ear Protection (Earplugs / Earmuffs): Required in areas with noise levels above 85 dB (e.g., near conveyor systems, compactors)
- Dust Masks / Respirators: Required in dusty environments, chemical storage areas, or when handling fine particulate goods
- Chemical-Resistant Gloves and Apron: Required when handling hazardous materials, cleaning agents, batteries
- Face Shield: Required for HazMat handling and liquid chemical tasks
- Anti-Static Wrist Strap: Required when handling electronic components

2.3 PPE Inspection and Maintenance:
- PPE must be inspected before each use
- Damaged or worn PPE must be reported to supervisors and replaced immediately
- PPE is provided free of charge by the employer
- Workers must not modify or tamper with PPE


3. FORKLIFT AND MATERIALS HANDLING EQUIPMENT (MHE) SAFETY
-----------------------------------------------------------
Forklifts are the leading cause of serious warehouse injuries. Strict rules apply.

3.1 Forklift Operator Certification:
- Only certified and authorized operators are permitted to operate forklifts
- Operator certification must be renewed every 3 years or after an incident
- Operators must complete a daily pre-operation checklist before using equipment

3.2 Forklift Operating Rules:
- Maximum speed limits apply: 5 mph (8 km/h) in warehouse areas, 3 mph in pedestrian zones
- Forks must be lowered to travel height (6 inches / 15 cm from ground) when moving
- Never exceed the forklift's rated load capacity (visible on the data plate)
- Do not carry passengers on forklifts — only operator is permitted on vehicle
- Sound horn at all blind corners, intersections, and doorways
- Maintain clear visibility; if load blocks view, drive in reverse with a spotter
- No mobile phone use while operating powered equipment
- Forklifts must be parked with forks lowered, engine off, and handbrake applied

3.3 Pedestrian Safety:
- Pedestrian walkways must be clearly marked with yellow painted lines
- Pedestrians must never cross forklift aisles without visually confirming it is safe
- Pedestrians always have right of way at designated crossing points
- Forklift operators must give way to pedestrians at all times

3.4 MHE Daily Pre-Checks:
Before starting any shift, check:
- Tires: Inflation, wear, damage
- Forks: Cracks, bends, wear
- Mast: Chains, hydraulic fluid, smooth operation
- Lights: Operational (headlights, reverse alarm, strobe)
- Horn: Functional
- Battery (electric): Charge level
- Brakes: Operational
Any defect must be reported to maintenance and the vehicle must be tagged out of service.


4. RACKING AND SHELVING SAFETY
--------------------------------
Racking failures cause serious accidents including fatalities from collapsing racks.

4.1 Load Limits:
- Never exceed the Safe Working Load (SWL) displayed on racking labels
- Pallet weights must be verified before put-away
- Uneven or damaged pallets must not be placed on racking

4.2 Racking Inspection:
- Racking must be inspected weekly by trained personnel
- Annual formal inspection by a qualified rack inspector is mandatory
- Any damage (dents, bends, broken clips, leaning columns) must be reported immediately
- Damaged racking must be unloaded and barricaded until repaired

4.3 Forklift and Racking Interaction:
- Forklift drivers must approach racking slowly and square-on
- Racking damage caused by forklift impact must be reported immediately
- End-of-aisle racking must be fitted with rack end protectors

4.4 Height and Climbing Rules:
- Workers must never climb on racking shelves to retrieve goods
- Access to high locations requires an approved order picker, cherry picker, or ladder
- Portable ladders must be secured and must not be leaned against racking uprights


5. FIRE SAFETY
---------------
5.1 Fire Prevention:
- No smoking anywhere inside warehouse premises
- Flammable materials must be stored in designated fireproof storage areas
- Sprinkler system clearance: Minimum 18 inches (45 cm) of clearance must be maintained below all sprinkler heads at all times
- Electrical equipment must be switched off when not in use
- Charging stations for electric forklifts / batteries must have dedicated ventilation

5.2 Fire Evacuation:
- Fire evacuation routes must be clearly marked and never obstructed
- Emergency exits must remain unlocked and accessible at all times
- Fire assembly point must be known to all staff
- Fire drills conducted at least twice per year

5.3 Fire Extinguishers:
- Types: Class A (general), Class B (flammable liquids), Class C (electrical), Class D (metals)
- Fire extinguishers must be inspected monthly and serviced annually
- Staff must be trained in fire extinguisher use (PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
- Never use water extinguishers on electrical or chemical fires

5.4 Fire Reporting:
- Any fire, however small, must be reported immediately
- Raise fire alarm first; evacuation is the priority — do not fight a large fire


6. MANUAL HANDLING AND ERGONOMICS
------------------------------------
6.1 Safe Lifting Technique:
- Assess the load before lifting: weight, size, shape, grip points
- Never lift loads over 25 kg alone; team-lift or use mechanical aids
- Safe lifting posture: Stand close to load, feet shoulder-width apart, bend knees (not back), grip firmly, keep load close to body, lift smoothly
- Do not twist while carrying — turn using feet

6.2 Mechanical Aids:
- Use trolleys, pallet jacks, and conveyor belts whenever possible
- Push (don't pull) trolleys and carts
- Use handles, grip tape, or lifting straps for awkward loads

6.3 Ergonomic Workstation Setup:
- Packing stations must be at waist height to avoid bending
- Anti-fatigue mats should be provided at standing workstations
- Regular microbreaks (5 min every hour) reduce musculoskeletal risk
- Stock heavy items in the "golden zone" (between knee and shoulder height)


7. HAZARDOUS MATERIALS (HAZMAT) HANDLING
------------------------------------------
7.1 HAZMAT Classification:
Hazardous materials are classified per DG (Dangerous Goods) regulations:
- Class 1: Explosives
- Class 2: Gases (flammable, non-flammable, toxic)
- Class 3: Flammable liquids
- Class 4: Flammable solids
- Class 5: Oxidizers and organic peroxides
- Class 6: Toxic and infectious substances
- Class 7: Radioactive materials
- Class 8: Corrosives
- Class 9: Miscellaneous dangerous goods

7.2 HazMat Storage Rules:
- Hazardous materials must be stored in designated, segregated areas
- Incompatible materials must not be stored together (e.g., oxidizers with flammables)
- Secure, ventilated, locked storage cabinets for flammables and corrosives
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) must be available for all hazardous materials
- Secondary containment (spill bunds) required for liquids

7.3 Spill Response:
- Evacuate the immediate area
- Alert supervisor and safety officer immediately
- Use correct spill kit for the material type
- Do not attempt to clean up large chemical spills without training and full PPE

7.4 HazMat Labeling:
- All hazardous goods must display correct UN/GHS hazard labels
- Labels must be clearly visible and not obscured


8. WORKING AT HEIGHT
---------------------
- Any work above 2 meters is classified as "work at height"
- Appropriate equipment must be used: order pickers, scissor lifts, cherry pickers
- Fall arrest harnesses must be worn when working in elevated platforms
- Never lean precariously over the edge of elevated platforms
- Platforms must be inspected before each use
- No working at height during wet, icy, or high-wind conditions


9. HOUSEKEEPING AND FLOOR SAFETY
----------------------------------
- Warehouse floors must be swept and cleaned regularly
- Spills must be cleaned immediately and "Wet Floor" signs erected
- Aisles and emergency exits must remain clear at all times
- Cardboard, plastic wrap, and packing materials must be disposed of in designated waste areas
- Broken pallets must be removed and not reused
- No personal items (bags, coats) left in working aisles


10. INCIDENT REPORTING
-----------------------
- All incidents (accidents, near misses, unsafe conditions) must be reported immediately
- Even minor injuries must be reported and recorded
- Incident report forms must be completed within 24 hours
- Near-miss reporting is encouraged — near misses are accident warnings
- Investigation and corrective action follow every incident


11. VISITOR AND CONTRACTOR SAFETY
-----------------------------------
- All visitors must sign in at the reception and be issued a visitor badge
- Visitors must be accompanied by an authorized staff member at all times
- Visitors must wear PPE (Hi-Vis vest minimum) while in warehouse areas
- Contractors must complete a site induction before beginning any work
- No unauthorized access to dock areas, racking zones, or HazMat areas
